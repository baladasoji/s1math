What Students Should Be Able To Do

This page outlines the core learning objectives and competences for S1 Mathematics. Each objective describes a specific skill or understanding students should develop, organized by cognitive level from foundational to advanced.

🔢 Numbers Competences

Classify Numbers into Different Types

Foundation

Students can identify and categorize numbers as natural, whole, integer, rational, or irrational.

  • Recognize natural and whole numbers
  • Identify integers and their properties
  • Distinguish between rational and irrational numbers
  • Use appropriate symbols (∈, ⊆) correctly
Bloom's Level: Remember, Understand

Perform Operations with All Number Types

Application

Students can add, subtract, multiply, and divide with fractions, decimals, and signed numbers accurately.

  • Execute operations with whole numbers and integers
  • Perform fraction operations including simplification
  • Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Apply order of operations correctly
Bloom's Level: Apply

📐 Algebra Competences

Form and Simplify Algebraic Expressions

Foundation

Students can write algebraic expressions from words or patterns and simplify by collecting like terms.

  • Translate word phrases to expressions
  • Identify and combine like terms
  • Remove brackets using distribution
  • Simplify complex expressions systematically
Bloom's Level: Understand, Apply

Solve Linear Equations

Application

Students can solve linear equations with one variable using inverse operations and balancing methods.

  • Solve simple linear equations (ax = b)
  • Solve multi-step equations (ax + b = c)
  • Solve equations with variables on both sides
  • Verify solutions and check reasonableness
Bloom's Level: Apply

🎨 Geometry Competences

Identify and Classify Geometric Shapes

Foundation

Students can recognize and classify 2D and 3D shapes by their properties and characteristics.

  • Identify polygons and name them
  • Classify triangles by sides and angles
  • Classify quadrilaterals by properties
  • Identify and describe 3D solids
Bloom's Level: Remember, Understand

Calculate Perimeter, Area, Surface Area, and Volume

Application

Students can apply appropriate formulas to measure 2D and 3D figures accurately.

  • Calculate perimeter of polygons
  • Find areas of common 2D shapes
  • Calculate surface area of prisms and cylinders
  • Determine volume of solids
Bloom's Level: Apply

⚡ Set Theory Competences

Represent Sets Using Different Notations

Foundation

Students can describe sets using roster notation, set-builder notation, and interval notation appropriately.

  • Write sets in roster form
  • Express sets using set-builder notation
  • Use interval notation for number sets
  • Distinguish between different notations
Bloom's Level: Understand

Perform Set Operations

Application

Students can perform union, intersection, complement, and difference operations on sets.

  • Find union of sets (A ∪ B)
  • Find intersection of sets (A ∩ B)
  • Find complement of sets (A')
  • Apply operations to solve problems
Bloom's Level: Apply

Core Mathematical Competences

Beyond topic-specific skills, students should develop these overarching mathematical competences:

Problem Solving

Identify problems, devise strategies, and implement solutions systematically.

Communication

Explain mathematical thinking clearly using proper notation and language.

Reasoning

Use logical thinking to justify conclusions and validate arguments.

Connections

Link mathematics to real-world contexts and other subject areas.

Representation

Use multiple forms (equations, graphs, tables, diagrams) to represent ideas.

Persistence

Approach challenges with strategies and learn from mistakes.
