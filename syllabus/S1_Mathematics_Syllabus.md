## Pedagogical Principles for S1

The S1 course is a transitional year from primary mathematics to secondary in that tangible mathematical concepts first met in primary will become increasingly more abstract in future years in secondary. However, it is important that the challenge encountered from P5 to S1 is as smooth as possible.

- **Focus on Core Concepts**: Limiting new mathematical concepts while building on core ideas met in primary
- **Extension Activities**: Plenty of time for extension activities using horizontal rather than vertical approaches
- **Functions Introduction**: The idea of functions will be introduced in S1 using pictorial sequences, laying foundation for more abstract ideas in future years
- **Inquiry-Based Learning**: Investigations, making abstractions and modelling are essential with maximum activity by pupils themselves
- **Technology Integration**: Use of spreadsheets, CAS software, DGS, and other digital tools to support and promote understanding

## YEAR S1 TOPIC: NUMBERS

### Numbers

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand the difference between natural and integer numbers
- Plot points on a plane (only with integer coordinates)
- Understand absolute value of an integer number in relation to the number line using notation, e.g., |−3| = 3
- Know how to compare and order integer numbers
- Use the transitivity property of > and <
- Understand prime numbers using the concept of factors and divisors
- Understand that all natural numbers can be written as a product of primes (prime factor decomposition)
- Know the criteria of divisibility by 2, 3, 5 and 10
- Use prime numbers to find the Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) and the Highest Common Factor (HCF)

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- How negative numbers came around due to money and the idea of debt
- Introduce a CAD software for plotting points
- Heights on a map
- Loans/debts and savings
- Criteria of divisibility by 9, 11, 25, 50 and 100
- Sieve of Eratosthenes to determine prime numbers or cryptography
- Worded problems with runners, LCM/HCF applications

### Operations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Calculate by adding and subtracting integers and decimals
- Calculate by multiplying and dividing two integers
- Understand the importance of 0 and 1
- Apply the order of operations rules, brackets included, to make calculations

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Apply on a calculator
- History of the 0 (India)
- The area model for calculating 12 × 23

### Fractions

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand a fraction is a ratio of two integers
- Understand that different fractions can be equivalent and simplify fractions
- Know how to convert a fraction to a decimal (terminating decimals) and vice-versa
- Order fractions and decimals on the number line

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Importance of fractions – historically for sharing food
- Show equivalent fractions (e.g., 8/12 = 10/15)
- Find fraction functions on a calculator

## YEAR S1 TOPIC: ALGEBRA

### Formulae, Tables, Graphs

#### Learning Objectives:
- Use pictorial sequences (arithmetic only)
- Create rules to determine the next term of an arithmetic sequence
- Understand how to create a general rule for an arithmetic sequence
- Know how to substitute into a general rule to find the value of any term
- Create a table of values from a pictorial sequence
- Plot the sequence from a table of values
- Simplify equivalent expressions (e.g., a + a + a = 3a)
- Solve simple linear equations up to the form ax + b = cx + d

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Pupils draw the next pattern using matches
- Create rule: "each time I add 4"
- General rule: 4n + 1
- Find the 100th step
- Use spreadsheets to find rules and determine the nth term
- Use CAS software or photo-recognition software on smartphones to solve equations

## YEAR S1 TOPIC: GEOMETRY

### 3D Shapes

#### Learning Objectives:
- Recognise, draw or sketch top and side views of 3D shapes (prisms, cylinders, pyramids, cones and spheres)
- Classify 3D shapes according to: faces, edges, vertices; parallel and perpendicular faces and edges; curved or flat faces
- Draw 3D shapes using CAD software and by hand (only cubes and cuboids by hand)
- Recognise and construct nets (prisms and pyramids)

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Investigate and compare similarities and differences between 3D models
- Use objects built of small cubes
- Create different solids with a given volume
- 'Euler's formula' of Platonic solids
- Pupils investigate different types of packaging

### 2D Shapes

#### Learning Objectives:
- Recognise and name 2D shapes: triangles (equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right angled), quadrilaterals, regular polygons up to decagon, circle
- Classify 2D shapes with respect to: parallelism, perpendicularity, or equality of sides
- Know how to measure and construct line segments and angles using protractor and set-square
- Know how to construct triangles given: SSS, SAS/SSA, ASA
- Use compass directions and distances to find a location
- Develop the concept of an angle by making turns

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Find structures in the real world
- Make a floor tiling out of shapes
- Use CAD software for construction
- Use straws to investigate triangle construction
- Explore the sum of angles of a triangle
- Use with maps
- Turns from N to E is 90 degrees
- Investigate triangle sum on the globe

### Measurements

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand the distinction between a geometrical object and its measure
- Estimate and measure lengths using appropriate units
- Convert between different unit systems (metric and imperial)
- Develop the concept of area by estimation strategies using unit squares
- Recall and apply formulae to calculate perimeters and areas of squares and rectangles
- Develop the concept of volume by counting unit cubes in a cuboid
- Know and convert units of lengths and mass (from milli to kilo)

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Measurement of the world and units
- Different unit systems
- Fits small squares in a big square
- Fits small cubes in a big cube
- Very small and large units (Nano, Giga, etc.)

## YEAR S1 TOPIC: SET THEORY

### Sets

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand basic concepts of a set, elements, universal set, empty set, union, intersection and complement
- Draw Venn diagrams and use them to show logical relationships
- Use appropriately the symbols ∈, ∉, ∩, ∪ and ⊂

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Set of evens and odds, set of primes
- Venn diagrams showing relationships between quadrilaterals with different properties

## Assessment

Assessment is based on six mathematical competences:

1. **Knowledge and Comprehension** - Understanding of mathematical terms, symbols and principles
2. **Methods** - Carrying out mathematical processes
3. **Problem Solving** - Translating problems into mathematical symbols and reasoning
4. **Interpretation** - Drawing conclusions from information
5. **Communication** - Presenting reasoning using mathematical terminology
6. **Digital Competence** - Using technology appropriately

### Attainment Levels
- **A (9.0-10)**: Excellent
- **B (8.0-8.9)**: Very Good
- **C (7.0-7.9)**: Good
- **D (6.0-6.9)**: Satisfactory
- **E (5.0-5.9)**: Sufficient
- **F (3.0-4.9)**: Weak/Failed
- **FX (0-2.9)**: Very Weak/Failed
