## Pedagogical Principles for S2

In the specific case of the S2 course, mathematics is at a more formal level where many models can be used to give pupils (visual) support, e.g., the area model, a ratio table, tables as intermediary tool for creating a graph.

- **Number Focus**: Still much attention is given to the Number topic and pupils' understanding of the number system
- **Algebraic Skills**: More time is scheduled to develop algebraic skills, where investigations of number and geometric patterns play an important role
- **Transition from Words to Variables**: Pupils move from word formulae to formulae using a variable
- **Abstract Mathematics**: The mathematics is becoming more abstract compared to S1
- **2D and 3D Investigation**: The investigation of 2D and 3D shapes is a base for development of more formal mathematics concepts in years that follow
- **Formula Understanding**: It is more important that a pupil understands which formula to apply and why it works rather than memorising the formula

## YEAR S2 TOPIC: NUMBERS

### Rounding Numbers

#### Learning Objectives:
- Round the answer to a certain number of decimals or a number of significant figures

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Make the difference between accuracy of measurement and precision in physics

### Fractions

#### Learning Objectives:
- Convert two fractions to equivalent fractions with the same denominator

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Slicing a cake or a pizza

### Operations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Calculate by adding and subtracting rational numbers
- Calculate by multiplying and dividing rational numbers

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Apply to real world solutions (worded questions)

### Powers

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand the concept of factors and use the power notation with a positive base
- Recall and use the following power rules:
  - a^m × a^n = a^(m+n)
  - a^m ÷ a^n = a^(m-n)
  - (a^m)^n = a^(m×n)
  - (a × b)^m = a^m × b^m
  - (a ÷ b)^m = a^m ÷ b^m
  - Limitation: m and n are natural numbers
- Simplify expressions with powers

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Investigate similarities and differences between shorter notations (4 + 4 + 4 vs. 4 × 4 × 4)

### Order of Operations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Apply the order of operations rules, brackets and powers included, to make calculations

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Apply rules on calculators and try them on simple calculator apps on a smartphone

### Percentages

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand and know the relationship between fractions and percentages: 1/100 part = 1%
- Discover relationships between fractions and percentages
- Understand and use the relationship between percentages and fractions to find a percentage of a number

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- History of percentage notation and the % sign
- Apply to real world situations (worded questions)

### Ratios

#### Learning Objectives:
- Generate equivalent ratios supported by a context (e.g., recipes) using a ratio table
- Construct a scale line for a map or object
- Construct scale drawings
- Understand the scale ratio of a model and maps
- Solve problems where ratios (part to part) or proportions (part to whole) are involved

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Ratios in daily life; '... per ...' problems
- Scale line; ratio table
- Investigate the scale ratio of toy models and pictures in a Biology book
- Project with Integrated Science: create a model of the sun-earth-moon-system
- In context situations, compare ratios

## YEAR S2 TOPIC: ALGEBRA

### Formulae, Tables, Graphs

#### Learning Objectives:
- Interpret a time-distance graph
- Create a graph from a table and vice versa
- Investigate and interpret linear situations by a given graph or table
- Investigate and interpret quadratic situations by a given graph or table
- Recognise and plot graphs of linear and quadratic relationships of one variable
- Model situations or procedures by translating them into (word) formulae and by using tables and/or graphs

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Review and extend from the discrete model seen in S1 to a continuous model now
- Investigate linear relations from economics (total cost and price per item)
- Use direct proportion y = kx (converting units, currency)
- e.g., circumference and area of 'growing' squares
- Plot with a CAD system
- Use formulae, graphs and tables as tools to model, investigate and compare situations (salary, rent, phone call)

### Expressions and Equations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand when and how algebraic expressions can be simplified by combining like terms
- Expand a single bracket expression such as a(b + c)
- Solve simple linear equations up to the form ax + b = cx + d where a, b, c, d and x are rational numbers
- Extend to simplifying expressions such as a(bx + c) + d(ex + f)

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- The area model can be used to expand brackets
- Extending to simplifying complex expressions with brackets

## YEAR S2 TOPIC: GEOMETRY

### 2D Shapes

#### Learning Objectives:
- Investigate, recognise, classify and name quadrilaterals and triangles with respect to: parallel sides, perpendicular sides, equality of sides

### Lines and Angles

#### Learning Objectives:
- Construct lines and angles using ruler and compass: parallel lines, perpendicular lines, perpendicular bisectors of lines, bisect an angle
- Construct these lines and angles with an appropriate technological tool

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Using paper folding to create these lines and shapes
- Finding the loci equidistant from two points (e.g., using a map)
- Euler straight line

### Symmetry

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand the concept of symmetry
- Identify symmetry in shapes, the line(s) of reflection, the centre of symmetry

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Rangoli and Escher artworks
- Symmetry in nature (faces, organs, mirrors, …)

### Measurement - Quadrilaterals and Triangles

#### Learning Objectives:
- Recall and apply appropriate formula to calculate the area of quadrilaterals (including rhombus, parallelogram, kite and trapezium) and triangles
- Calculate perimeters and areas of compound shapes

### Measurement - Circle

#### Learning Objectives:
- Recall and apply appropriate formula to calculate the perimeter of a circle and area of a disk using π

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Investigation of the ratio between diameter and circumference
- History of π

### Measurement - Area and Volume

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand how to convert units for area (mm², cm², dm², m²)
- Understand how to convert units for volume (mm³, cm³, dm³, m³; ml, cl, dl, l, hl and 1 dm³ = 1 litre)
- Use appropriate units for area and volume when solving practical problems
- Investigate the relationship of area and volume of enlarged or reduced shapes

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Investigations of surface areas of packages/boxes
- Investigations of volumes of packages/boxes
- Explain why small children cool off faster than adults or mice than elephants

## YEAR S2 TOPIC: STATISTICS AND PROBABILITY

### Descriptive Statistics

#### Learning Objectives:
- Set up and carry out a simple survey to collect data
- Use tallying and frequency tables grouping data together
- Display (collected) data, construct and interpret statistical diagrams: line graphs, frequency diagrams, pie charts
- Investigate two sets of data making up measures to compare and make conclusions

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Experiments with temperature over time, display the data, draw conclusions
- Investigations: collect data, display the data, draw conclusions
- Survey
- Appreciate the difference between discrete and continuous data
- Collect data in Science experiments
- Pupils may informally use the idea of mean, median, mode, range, …

## Assessment

Assessment is based on six mathematical competences:

1. **Knowledge and Comprehension** - Understanding of mathematical terms, symbols and principles
2. **Methods** - Carrying out mathematical processes
3. **Problem Solving** - Translating problems into mathematical symbols and reasoning
4. **Interpretation** - Drawing conclusions from information
5. **Communication** - Presenting reasoning using mathematical terminology
6. **Digital Competence** - Using technology appropriately

### Attainment Levels
- **A (9.0-10)**: Excellent
- **B (8.0-8.9)**: Very Good
- **C (7.0-7.9)**: Good
- **D (6.0-6.9)**: Satisfactory
- **E (5.0-5.9)**: Sufficient
- **F (3.0-4.9)**: Weak/Failed
- **FX (0-2.9)**: Very Weak/Failed

### Assessment Methods
- Written questions for content knowledge
- Open questions for competences such as construction and explanation
- Design projects and assignments
- Experimental inquiry and investigations
- Digital competence through spreadsheets and data analysis
