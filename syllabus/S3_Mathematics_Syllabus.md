## Pedagogical Principles for S3

In the specific case of the S3 pupils, mathematics reaches a more formal level, especially in the Algebra and Geometry topics. However, some pupils may be unable to express the correct solution in a formal way.

- **Formal Mathematics Level**: Mathematics becomes more formal, especially in Algebra and Geometry
- **Visual Support Models**: It might be helpful to refer to models that can be used to give pupils (visual) support (area model, ratio table, tables as intermediary tool, geometric software)
- **Differentiation**: Pupils who can work more easily at a former level should be challenged to solve more complex problems
- **Less Number Focus**: There is a little less attention for the Number strand because many computations are integrated in the Algebra and Geometry strand
- **Statistics Beginning**: The Statistics and Probability strand starts with counting problems (paths and arrangements), which will be formalized as probability in Year 4 and 5

## YEAR S3 TOPIC: NUMBERS

### Operations - Roots

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand and use the square root of a number
- Use a calculator to find approximations of square roots
- Cube roots (make connections between the volume of a cube and its side length)

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Make connections between the area of a square and its side length

### Operations - Powers

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand and use powers with a negative base

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Pico, Nano, Micro, Mega, Giga, Tera, Peta

### Scientific Notation

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand and use the scientific notation

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Investigate the scientific notation on a calculator

### Order of Operations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Apply the order of operations rules, brackets, powers and square roots included, to make calculations

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Investigations how well a calculator can apply these rules

### Inverse

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand the additive inverse and the multiplicative inverse of a number

### Percentages and Ratios

#### Learning Objectives:
- Calculate a percentage of a number, a percentage increase and decrease
- Use the relationship between percentages and ratios to solve problems

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- A percent bar can be used to give visual support
- Project with Integrated Science: design a healthy, varied and balanced menu for a week

## YEAR S3 TOPIC: ALGEBRA

### Functions

#### Learning Objectives:
- Identify linear functions: Start to use the notation x → ax + b
- In situations interpret gradients and intercepts of graphs of linear functions graphically
- Solve problems involving direct and inverse proportion, including graphical and algebraic representations

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Plot functions on a CAD software
- Use compound units such as speed, unit pricing and density to solve problems

### Expressions and Equations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand the difference between an expression, an equation and a formula
- Manipulate formula of the following type:
  - a = bc, b = a/c
  - a = b + c, b = a − c
  - a = b², |b| = √a
- Understand when and how algebraic expressions can be manipulated to maintain equivalence by combining like terms
- Expand and simplify a bracket expression such as a(b + c) and (a + b)(c + d)
- Factorise ab + ac

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Circumference, area and volume
- Speed, density, …
- The area model can be introduced to expand brackets
- a and b are real numbers

#### Expand and Factorise (Identities)

#### Learning Objectives:
- Expand and factorise:
  - (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
  - (a − b)² = a² − 2ab + b²
  - (a + b)(a − b) = a² − b²
  - where a and b are fractions and/or variables
- Use these to calculate examples such as:
  - 103² = (100 + 3)²
  - 99² = (100 − 1)²
  - 51 × 49 = (50 + 1)(50 − 1)
- Simplify expressions containing brackets, fractions and combinations of both
- Simplify expressions containing even powers

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Where a and b are real numbers

#### Solve Linear Equations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Solve linear equations up to the form a(bx ± c) = d(ex ± f) where a, b, c, d, e, f and x (unknown) are rational numbers

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Use of a CAS software or formula recognition software

## YEAR S3 TOPIC: GEOMETRY

### 2D - Angles

#### Learning Objectives:
- Identify equal angles: vertically opposite angles, alternate interior and exterior angles, corresponding angles
- Prove the result of the sum of the angles of a triangle

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Prove the result of the sum of the angles of a triangle

### 2D - Transformations

#### Learning Objectives:
- Understand and apply transformations: translations, reflections, rotations, enlargements (positive scale factor)

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Introduce the concepts of scalar and vector quantities and how the properties are affected or not by the transformations
- Investigate how area is affected by an enlargement

### 2D - Ratios in Right Angle Triangles

#### Learning Objectives:
- Investigate and compare the steepness of different slopes
  - **Limitation**: do not introduce trigonometric functions and avoid using sin, cos and tan as this will be met in future years
- Use ratios to find the height of an object

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Who has designed the best paper plane? How can you compare them? Investigate how to determine the glide ratio of a paper plane
- Investigate and compare different glide ratios
- Construct right angled triangles to create a table showing glide angles (10°, 20°, 30°, …) and glide ratio of the two rectangle sides
- The height of a tower is 50 meters. How long is its shadow on June 21st? And on December 21st?
- Find the height of an object given the angle of the sunrays and its shadow length (Eratosthenes)
- Find the circumference of the Earth (Eratosthenes)

### Measurement - Area

#### Learning Objectives:
- Recall and apply appropriate formula to calculate surface areas of solids restricted to prisms
- Calculate surface areas of cylinders

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Surface area investigations

### Measurement - Volume

#### Learning Objectives:
- Recall and apply appropriate formula to calculate volumes of solids restricted to prisms, cylinders, pyramids, cones and spheres

### Measurement - Area and Volume

#### Learning Objectives:
- Recall and apply appropriate formula for area and volume to calculate length when given the area or volume
- Manipulate the formulae: recall and apply appropriate formula to calculate the area (face or base) when given a volume
- Investigate relationships between area and volume, the most volume with the least surface area, etc.

## YEAR S3 TOPIC: STATISTICS AND PROBABILITY

### Descriptive Statistics

#### Learning Objectives:
- Calculate the mean, the median and the mode from a data set
- Develop strategies and use the mean, the median and/or the mode to compare two sets of data to draw conclusions
- Calculate the mean, median and mode from a frequency table

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Use data from scientific sources

### Combinatorics - Number of Paths, Arrangements

#### Learning Objectives:
- Use systematic strategies for counting such as sample space diagrams and tree diagrams to list all outcomes
  - **Limitation**: do not use these for probability
- Count different arrangements

#### Key Contexts & Activities:
- Count different arrangements (e.g., to choose a menu: 2 starters, 3 main courses, 2 desserts)
- Different routes to go from A to B on a grid
- Planning a seating arrangement
- **Limitation**: avoid nCr/nPr and factorials

## Assessment

Assessment is based on six mathematical competences:

1. **Knowledge and Comprehension** - Understanding of mathematical terms, symbols and principles
2. **Methods** - Carrying out mathematical processes
3. **Problem Solving** - Translating problems into mathematical symbols and reasoning
4. **Interpretation** - Drawing conclusions from information
5. **Communication** - Presenting reasoning using mathematical terminology
6. **Digital Competence** - Using technology appropriately

### Attainment Levels
- **A (9.0-10)**: Excellent
- **B (8.0-8.9)**: Very Good
- **C (7.0-7.9)**: Good
- **D (6.0-6.9)**: Satisfactory
- **E (5.0-5.9)**: Sufficient
- **F (3.0-4.9)**: Weak/Failed
- **FX (0-2.9)**: Very Weak/Failed

### Assessment Methods
- Written questions for content knowledge
- Open questions for competences such as explanation and argumentation
- Design projects and assignments
- Experimental inquiry and investigations
- Digital competence through spreadsheets, data analysis, and modelling
